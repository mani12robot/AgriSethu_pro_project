Organic Farming

100% chemical-free agriculture for a healthier planet

Pure Organic Farming

At Agrisethu, organic farming isn't just a method—it's our commitment to the planet, farmers, and consumers. We practice 100% chemical-free agriculture, using only natural inputs, traditional wisdom, and modern technology to produce premium organic crops.

Our expert farmers are trained in sustainable practices that not only yield healthy produce but also regenerate soil health, conserve water, and support biodiversity. Every crop we grow meets international organic certification standards.

Complete Farming Cycle

From soil preparation to harvest - our comprehensive approach

Soil Preparation

Natural composting, green manuring, and bio-fertilizers to enrich soil with organic matter and beneficial microorganisms.

Seed Selection

Certified organic, non-GMO seeds chosen based on soil type, climate, and market demand using our AI prediction system.

Planting

Strategic planting schedules, crop rotation, and companion planting to maximize yield and natural pest resistance.

Irrigation

Efficient drip irrigation and water conservation techniques ensuring optimal moisture without waste or runoff.

Pest Management

Natural pest control using beneficial insects, neem-based solutions, and bio-pesticides—zero chemical pesticides.

Harvesting

Timely harvesting at peak ripeness, careful handling, and immediate quality checks to ensure premium produce.

Quality Monitoring System

Rigorous checks at every stage ensuring premium organic produce

Field Monitoring

  • Weekly Inspections: Regular field visits by quality supervisors
  • Soil Testing: Periodic analysis to maintain organic standards
  • Crop Health Tracking: Early detection of issues and natural remedies
  • Input Verification: Ensuring only certified organic inputs are used
  • Documentation: Complete records of all farming activities

Harvest Quality Control

  • Visual Inspection: Size, color, and appearance standards
  • Residue Testing: Zero tolerance for chemical contamination
  • Grading System: Classification based on quality parameters
  • Traceability: Complete farm-to-market tracking system
  • Certification: Third-party organic certification verification
